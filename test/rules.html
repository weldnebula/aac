Rules and Conventions (ways of doing things)

Welcome to All About Computers! This is a resource for normal people to learn how computer technology works. All About Computers does not use cookies, and has no advertising.

In explaining computer technology on this site, we sometimes skip over historical and scientific details that a typical computer science course would include. Instead, we stick to the essential knowledge that will demystify computers for you, and leave it to you to investigate those underlying subjects in more detail if you want to.

How we got here

Computer technology has been built up over the years as a product of two primary influences:

  • The nature of logic, mathematics, and the physical world â€” electricity, magnetism, optics, mechanics, materials, and so on.
  • The choices that key industry players have made in deciding how to take advantage of the behavior of logic, mathematics, and the physical world.
    One key choice was the decision to use the binary number system as the basis for computer logic. (And no, you don't have to understand binary numbers to use a computer!)
